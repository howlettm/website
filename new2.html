Michael Howlett (Professor) BSocSci.(Hon)(Ott), MA(Br Col), PhD (Queen's) specializes in public policy analysis, Canadian political economy, and Canadian resource and environmental policy. He is co-author of ' Studying Public Policy: Policy Cycles and Policy Subsystems (2003 and 1995), In Search of Sustainability: British Columbia Forest Policy in the 1990s (2001), The Political Economy of Canada: An Introduction (1999 & 1992) and Canadian Natural Resource and Environmental Policy: Political Economy and Public Policy (1997). He has edited Canadian Forest Policy: Adapting To Change (2001) and coedited The Provincial State In Canada (1992 and 2000); Regional Innovation Systems: The North American Experience (1998); Policy Studies in Canada: The State of the Art (1996), and The Puzzles of Power: Introductory Readings in Political Science (1994 & 1998). His articles have been published in numerous professional journals in Canada, the United States, Europe, Brazil, New Zealand and Australia. Dr. Howlett is currently English Language Co-editor of the Canadian Journal of Political Science (2002-2005) and is Burnaby Mountain Chair in the Department.
